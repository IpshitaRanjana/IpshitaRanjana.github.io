DFT Study of Solid Electrolyte for LI-ION Battery Application.

Density functional theory is used to investigate the atomic, electronic and ionic properties of solid electrolytes in their pristine and doped structures. In addition, the effect of doping Hafnium (Hf) and Titanium (Ti) in LLZO was compared through atomic, electronic and ionic properties.

  • Increasing the amount Ti dopant concentration can help increase the ionic conductivity by reducing the activation energy to 0.48 eV due to extreme lattice distortion.
  • The slightly smaller ionic radius of Hf compared to Zr can help distort the lattice parameter to a more cubic structure which has a better ionic conductivity compared to pristine LLZO at room temperature.
  • This study sheds light on the atomic level design such as tuning structure and ionic conductivity of the solid electrolytes without compromising chemical stability.
